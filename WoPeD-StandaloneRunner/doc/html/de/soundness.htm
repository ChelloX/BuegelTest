Soundness

The most important property of workflow nets is soundness, implying that the Petri net model is not only structurally well-formed (as covered by the definition of workflow nets itself) but also behaviorally well-formed. This means that

  • the only deadlock is caused by the final marking (one token on the output place and no token elsewhere)
  • every complete run - i. e. every execution of the net starting from the initial marking (one token on the input place and no tokens elsewhere) eventually leads to the final marking
  • for every transition there exists at least one execution it occurs in
