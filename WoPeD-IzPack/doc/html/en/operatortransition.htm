Operator transitions

The class of workflow nets provides a set of four operator transitions to express branching situations:

  • AND-split transitions    are starting points of a parallel branching
  • XOR-split transitions    are starting points of an alternative branching
  • AND-join transitions    are closing points of a parallel branching
  • XOR-join transitions    are closing points of an alternative branching
  • XOR-split-join transitions    are closing and starting points of alternative branching
  • AND-split-join transitions    are closing and starting points of parallel branching
  • XOR-join-AND-split transitions    are closing points of an alternative branching and starting points of parallel branching
  • AND-join-XOR-split transitions    are closing points of a parallel branching and starting points of an alternative branching

Additionally, subprocess transitions   can be used to refer to embedded suprocess definitions, defining hierarchically structured Petri Nets. This feature makes it possible to handle large Petri nets even if their graphical representation is exceeding the size of the editor window.
