Simulation

Simulation is a method of quantitative analysis where a business process model is executed and statistical data gathered.

The business process model must give the average service time of transitions, the split probabilities of XOR splits and the group / role signature of each transition.

When you open the simulation dialog you will see a lot of information. Just follow it in the normal reading direction from left to right and from top to bottom.

At first you have to give the average number of new cases per period λ, then the period π itself. You should choose the appropriate time unit for your period. Next you select the kind of queuing discipline you prefer. After that give the number of simulation runs. This specifies how often a simulation is repeated. The results shown in the tables below are the average values of all simulations runs. Next you may change the termination rule. You can choose between exiting when at least λ cases were finished, when the period has elapsed or both. Selecting both rules means its logical disjunction. Then the simulation stops when the first of the two constraints is true.

The last two adjustments concern the probability distribution of the arrival process and the service processes. You can choose a constant distribution with parameter ς where each value in the interval [(1-ς) λ, (1+ς) λ] around mean λ or average service time μ respectively has the same probability. ς must be an integer between 0 and 100. Or you choose a Poisson distribution with parameter λπ. The third alternative is the normal or Gaussian distribution with mean λ and the standard deviation σ which must be given as parameter.

If you prefer another standard time unit than 1 minute, which is the default, you can change it in the time model dialog by clicking on [Time model].

After giving all necessary information you can start the simulation by clicking on [Start]. A wait dialog appears until all operations are done. Then the tables show the updated results. In addition three more buttons are activated. In the tables you can see the statistics of the whole process and each transition, here called servers, and the utilization ratios of all resource objects.

The column titles of the first table have the following meaning:

  • L      the average number of cases in the system

  • Lq     the average number of cases waiting

  • Ls     the average number of cases being served

  • W     the average completion time of cases (including service time and wait time)

  • Wq    the average wait time of cases

By clicking on a button in the Details column another window opens and gives additional information.

After the simulation is done you can examine the protocol by clicking the [Protocol] button, export data into CSV files by clicking [Export] or have a look at a chart where the operation of each case by a certain resource at a particular server is shown by clicking on [Diagram].

With [Close] you can close the dialog.
