Coverability Graph Extras

Coverability

Computing a coverability graph can be very difficult to handle. Some graphs tend to get very big or are not really solvable in available time. There is a special case of coverability graphs that are not ending while computing them. Due to the unboundedness of a petri net. An unbounded petri net has places, that can get an infinite number of tokens.
To handle this situation and to still get the graph drawn you can compute a coverability graph. This graph is just like a normal reachability graph but it can handle unbounded places. It represents unbounded places by a "∞".

Edge Color Highlighting

Sometimes - in large coverability graphs - it can be difficult to see the incoming and outgoing edges of a marking. For this reason it is possible to click on a marking to highlight the surrounding edges. See screenshot below at mark 1.

Incoming edges are highlighted in red.

State-highlighting

You are also able to highlight the different states of a coverability graph. It will be colored in blue and also displayed in the editor window. Removing the state-highlighting will be done by double-click the left mouse-button in the background of the coverability graph or use the unsetbutton in the toolbar.

Attention: The editor is blocked while you have highlighted the coverability graph. You have to unmark the coverability graph if you want to use the editor again.
